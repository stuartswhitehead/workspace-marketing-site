Learn everything you need to know about Watson Workspace

In general, check out the Watson Workspace Help Center for help signing up and using Watson Workspace. There are also a ton of articles (and awesome search) providing guidance on pretty much everything you might want to know.

Who is using the preview?

Watson Workspace is being used by over two thousand IBMers and early adoption customers. It’s open to all IBMers, and anyone you invite, whether they work for IBM or not!

Can I use this with external customers?

Yes! You can send a direct message to anyone and add anyone to a space. If they’re using Workspace, you should be able to find them by just typing their name. If not, just add them by their email address. If they’re not already using Workspace, they’ll receive an email with instructions on how to sign up and join the conversation with you.

How do I invite people to the preview?

All IBMers have access to the preview, so they don’t need an invitation. Just send them a message or add them to a space! If they don’t show up in the typeahead, invite them by their email address and they’ll get an invitation with instructions on how to sign up.

Is this a TAP project?

No, Watson Workspace is designed and developed by IBMers in IBM Collaboration Solutions. Workspace is available for download on the TAP website, but the project was not created by TAP.

How do I join spaces?

Right now, we don’t yet support public spaces. All conversations are private to the people in a particular space. We are exploring the possibility of public, discoverable spaces for future iterations. If this is important to you and your work - vote for the idea here.

Can I discuss IBM Confidential information in Workspace?

Yes, just be sure to append an IC to the end of any space name where you are sharing IBM Confidential information so others in the team are aware. Check out this article for more information.

Where can I provide feedback?

We love to get feedback and ideas! If you have a suggestion or a request, or if you want to report an issue, contact us from the Help Center or from settings in the browser or mobile apps. You can also submit ideas to our idea forum or ask a question in our general discussion forum.

Where do I go if I have a question?

First, check the Help Center. Use search to check both the articles and the forums. If you can’t find an answer there, contact us from the Help Center. There’s a direct link to the contact form in each of our apps too in the user settings.

When can we expect new updates in Watson Workspace?

The Watson Workspace team employs continuous delivery so we are releasing updates to the Workspace apps all the time - sometimes new releases several times a day. The best way to keep up with what we are working on and what to expect soon is to follow our idea forum. Also keep an eye on our our "What’s new" article which we update whenever there are significant updates.

How is this different from Slack?

There are lots of similarities between Watson Workspace and Slack. Both tools recognized, as have many in the market, that conversations are key to how people work and collaborate. Conversations are the glue between the tools you are working in, the content you are sharing, and the decisions you are making. So yes, both Slack and Watson Workspace are persistent messaging platforms designed for teams, but we also think there are some key differences.

  • Slack positions their product as "all your communication in one place". Watson Workspace aims to be so much more than just the place to communicate; our north star is to use Watson technology to enable work. As our product evolves, you’ll definitely see a focus on not only communication, but creating content, connecting people, and driving towards outcomes.
  • Cognitive is core to Watson Workspace. Cognitive is not just an app or a bot but Watson Workspace has been built on Watson cognitive technology and learning from day one. We’ve got moments and action fulfillment and plenty of more plans to keep leveraging cognitive to make it easier to get your work done, manage the noise, and help you focus on what matters.
  • We know lots of people love Slack, and we aren’t trying to take them away from a tool that works for them, but even if some of your team is already using Slack, you can still give Watson Workspace a go. Add the Slack app to a space in Workspace, connect to a channel in Slack, and the conversation in Watson Workspace and the conversation in Slack become one. Messages sent in either place will show up in both apps.
  • The most unique difference between Slack and Watson Workspace? You have the opportunity to influence the design and development of our product - just contact us.

Why are we building this when Slack already exists?

IBM has long been a leader in the collaboration business. We acknowledge, along with Slack (and many others in this space), that conversations are the future of collaboration and moreover, the foundation of how most work happens. Our customers, however, are not just looking for a persistent chat app, but instead for an IBM solution that plays well with other IBM offerings, applies the same diligence to enterprise readiness - including globalization, compliance and security - that the IBM brand promises and takes advantage of the breadth of IBM’s portfolio by incorporating cognitive technology, workflow capabilities, profiles, document creation and more. This is what we are building with Watson Workspace and the Watson Work Services platform.

Is this based on Sametime?

No, this is a brand new offering, built on entirely new technology.

How is this different from Sametime?

We’ve been working hard to take what you love about Sametime to the next level with Watson Workspace. Here are two prime examples:

  • Sametime is great for synchronous conversations, but we know that conversations can be synchronous and asynchronous. In Watson Workspace, the conversations are persistent. You don’t have to start a new message every day with the same person or search through the history of your conversations day by day, and the best part, you can message someone even if they aren’t online.
  • Sametime has desktop and native mobile apps same as Watson Workspace, but with Watson Workspace you can be logged into multiple devices at the same time.

Watson Workspace doesn’t do everything that Sametime does and it won’t; we are definitely being very selective about the features we develop, but we also don’t think you should have to use multiple tools for similar purposes, so our goal is to meet the bar that keeps rising for work collaboration.

Should I use Watson Workspace or IBM Connections?

Both! IBM Connections and Watson Workspace serve unique purposes.

Use IBM Connections to:

  • Create communities for interest or broadcast or even large scale projects. Think "general awareness" to many.
  • Manage all your personal files.
  • Find expertise or view user profiles.
  • Publish pages of content for others to access or contribute to.

Use Watson Workspace to:

  • Create purposeful groups that need to work together on a regular basis.
  • Upload and share files, links, and urls to your team.
  • Keep up with real time activity from third party apps.
  • Privately message another person.

Because IBM Connections and Watson Workspace are extremely complimentary we are planning key integrations between the two apps so that you can work and share seamlessly from either app.

Can I create my own apps for Workspace?

Yes! Anyone can create an app and add it to spaces they’re in or submit it for review so it’s available to all spaces. You can even use our cognitive APIs to extend other business tools. Visit our developer experience for Watson Work Services to learn more about what you can do with our APIs and start creating your own custom integrations.

Can I get someone to do a demo?

If you’re interested in scheduling a demo for you or your team, just contact us.

When does Watson Workspace GA (general availability)?

Watson Workspace is currently expected to be generally available as a paid, enterprise offering in September 2017.

Work from anywhere, with anyone, on whatever needs to get done

Watson Workspace is at its best for teams that work together on a regular basis. It could be any kind of work that requires you to connect often, communicate frequently, and stay aligned regularly - but here are a few examples:

Project managers

Watson Workspace helps project managers efficiently run their projects without cutting corners. You can create spaces for your team to share project updates, files, urls, resources, and deliverables and get feedback in real time. Team members can communicate seamlessly so they are always up to speed with what’s coming next. And you can even create custom integrations to push updates from your favorite project management tool into Watson Workspace so you never miss a beat.

Sales

Watson Workspace is perfect for sales teams working together to help identify, move forward, and close opportunities. Create cross-team spaces to improve transparency and communication between sales, marketing, product development, and support. Use customer spaces to converse in real time with external clients and partners. Easily find the details of previous deals, decisions, and documents with search that finds what you need, no matter what space.

Executives

Executives typically oversee a lot of different projects and teams. Watson Workspace helps executives stay tuned into what matters, make decisions, and guide the people that count on them. As an executive you can quickly assemble team spaces to deliver business outcomes and stay connected with your employees no matter where they are. Even when you are away, it’s easy catch up on work that has transpired using moments, and then jump right back in to provide feedback and guidance without scheduling more meetings.

Developers

Watson Workspace makes it easy for developers and engineers to collaborate seamlessly together across time zones and around the world. You can create spaces to facilitate scrums and swats, or to communicate with design, ops, and product management from any device. You can even build your own custom integrations to track bug notifications, change requests, or system outages. Need to focus on the code? Catch up easily on what you missed using moments.
